# Embeddings from Prediction

Embeddings become meaningful because they help solve a task.

In language modeling, token embeddings are trained to predict tokens from context. In recommender systems, user and item embeddings are trained to predict interactions. In retrieval, query and document embeddings are trained to rank relevant documents above irrelevant ones.

Many embedding methods can also be understood as factorizing a large relationship matrix such as word-context co-occurrence, user-item interaction, or query-document relevance.
